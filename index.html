I. The Name
Hannah Flores says history sounds like "Nelson." Just Nelson.
Believing that a first name can carry the weight of an entire century. That two syllables can make meaning of everything that was and wasn't taken away.
II. The Allusion
The allusion to Nelson Mandela represents 27 years in prison.
That's not just a number, it's a scale Long enough for a child to be born And recite the rules of segregation. Within an increasingly divided nation.

III. Four Walls
Placed in a cell the size of a bathroom, He saw the outside world in rectangles Through window bars that were meant to keep in rather than let out.
They shrunk his world into 4 walls and a floor And made silence feel permanent To make resistance feel impractical and unsure.
Robben Island
IV. The Weapon But who knew that this confinement, This deliberate silencing, It could be a weapon.
1990
V. 1990 — The Release
So when he left the bars of Robben Island in 1990, Stepping into sunlight shredded from the inside, He rose to power as a president of change, Aiming to dismantle the apartheid without any rage.
1990 Release from Robben Island

After 27 years, 6 months, and 6 days
VI. The Stitching
Seeing a nation ripped apart by its former shadow. Nelson stitched everything back together.
He didn't use needles or scissors, But instead actions that speak much louder.
He chose forgiveness and unity. Bringing people together with his purity.
Nelson.
Just Nelson.
